Projects. A Wide Variety.

Throughout the past couple of years I was able to work on many different projects while in school, internships, and work. One of the first projects where I really applied GIS was a school project that mapped crime rates and types around campus. I contacted and worked with employees of the campus police to collect data about local crime and created heat maps and other visulizations to show trends. I contributed at my intership to mapping trends of crime rates around John Hopkin University for the Homewood Community Partners Initiave, created Towson University bus route maps that were published around campus bus stops, and support Eastern Shore Libraries using GIS through the Sparks Ignition Grant.
